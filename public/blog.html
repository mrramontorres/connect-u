Working with VCs by Viviana

Muse

Approached Muse more as an advisory meeting than an investment one. They focus on seed stage investment and we’re at Series F so there wasn’t going to be a proper pitch. Loved their insight on the consumer market. They are very hands on so I suggest only seed or even pre-seed stage startups approach them otherwise you may find yourself in a sticky situation. They are nice and knowledgeable but not for anything past Series A.

Better Ventures

We actually approached Better Ventures early on in our fundraising process so this information may be out of date. They didn’t end up writing us a check but it took months before we got a definite answer. We didn’t appreciate being left in the unknown. Not sure if it was actually something about our particular round and check size but we just didn’t work out. Also, their focus on sustainability is more environment than overall sustainability. If that’s not you then I suggest moving on.

Rock Health

Met with CEO and Managing Director, Bill Evans, and Business Developer Elaine Wang. Pitch went well though Bill tends to make subtle facial expressions when he is unclear about something. I would suggest you take time to address them before moving on. The questions post pitch were intense. Elaine drills you about the go to market strategy and unit economics. It can be intimidating but as long as you have them you should be fine. Bill focuses his questions on why Rock Health would be a good lead investor for you. Make sure you have a clear explanation. Overall it went well. They know Digital Health so if you’re in that space they are the ones to go to.
